# SubprimeSignal 

## Could We Have Seen the 2008 Housing Crash Coming?

### How This Started

I have this habit of watching movies on weekends. Nothing fancy,as usuali was just scrolling through whatever's available and picking something random. That's how I ended up with *The Big Short*.

Michael Burry socially awkward hedge fund manager, obsessed with patterns looks at mortgage data in 2005 and figures out what everyone else is missing. 

The mortgage market was rotting. Lenders were taking garbage loans and wrapping them into securities. Everyone was cashing in, so no one bothered to actually look.

Burry looked.

He went through the raw mortgage data. The ugly documents nobody else bothered to read. Payment histories. Credit spreads. The fine print. By 2005 he'd figured it out. The whole thing was going to explode. He shorted housing.

And he was right.

But here's what stuck with me: **Was any of this actually unpredictable?**

Not in some genius-investor way. Just practically speaking if you had the data and basic statistics, could you have spotted it? Before the market caught on?

That's what I'm trying to figure out with this project.
